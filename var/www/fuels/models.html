Environmental Consequences Toolkit

Direct launch:

  • WEPP Fuel Management Erosion Analysis (WEPP FuME)

    Estimates background erosion rates and compares sediment loads and erosion from wildfire, thinning, prescribed fire, buffer width effectiveness and low and high use road networks for a given topography. Soil and water databases are the same as those used for WEPP.

  • The Understory Response Model (URM)

    Predicts qualitative changes in shrub, form, and grass biomass at 1, 5, 10 year intervals caused by fuels treatment activities. Based on species-specific life history traits (life form, shade tolerance, etc) and site-specific effects (soil heating, bare mineral soil, etc).

  • The Wildlife Response Model (WiRM)

    Predicts qualitative changes in habitat and populations in response to fuels treatment activities. Uses species-habitat relationships and knowledge of which habitat features a given treatment will affect to identify effects on reproduction, food resources, predators and hazards.

  • Armillaria Response Tool (ART)

    Models qualitative risk of Armillaria root rot disease due to selected fuels treatments. Determines whether given habitat supports the disease, and if so, estimates degree of risk.

  • National Fire Plan Project Design and Consultation Process (FCP)

    Provides effects determinations for species listed under the Endangered Species Act and other species of concern (OR, ID, NV, UT, wMT, wWY).
