Environmental Consequences

The Environmental Consequences team provides fuel treatment planners with the means to estimate the environmental consequences of proposed fuel treatment activities in the dry, interior forests of the western U.S. on:

  • Air
  • Soil and Water
  • Flora
  • Fauna (terrestrial and aquatic)
  • Armillaria root disease

In addition to environmental consequences, other teams considered fire behavior, economic, and social issues. To access these pages please use the Applied Wildland Fire Research link below.

Dry interior forest types considered include Ponderosa Pine, interior Douglas-fir, and Lodgepole Pine.
