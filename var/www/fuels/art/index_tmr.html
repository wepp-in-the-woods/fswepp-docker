Fuels Planning: Root Disease Analyzer
Armillaria Response Tool


The Armillaria Response Tool assesses variability in climate and terrain within forest stands to more accurately predict potential risk of Armillaria root rot as a result of fuels treatments.

Root rot fungi and other diseases respond differently to forest disturbance, based on interactions among soil, topography, climate, tree species and disturbance type. Classification of forested parcels by habitat type accounts for some of this variability, but many stands were given a stand-wide plant association label that may encompass two or more ecologically different areas. Use of GIS and new unbiased plant community classification systems can reveal such significant within-stand heterogeneity. The Armillaria Response Tool (ART) assesses that variability to more accurately predict potential risk of Armillaria root rot as a result of fuels treatment in a stand.


As an alternative to running ART, which involves GIS analysis of local terrain and climate, a manager can simply provide the known habitat type classification of the stand in question and use our "crosswalk tables" to estimate potential risk of Armillaria root rot.  However, by choosing this "shortcut" method, it is implicit that the manager assumes all responsibility for the accuracy of the habitat type classification, the ecological homogeneity of the stand, and any management decisions that are made as a result of using the tool.  By continuing (clicking the "Continue" button), you acknowledge that you understand and accept this responsibility.
