Armillaria Response Tools -- Fuel Synthesis

Armillaria Response Tool
(Under development)
The Root Disease Analyzer -- Armillaria Response Tool (ART) can determine Armillaria root disease risk in Rocky Mountain Montane Conifer Forest biotic communities. It uses habitat type to identify sites with high and low risk that pathogenic Armillaria will be present, and indicates how fire planning (fuels management) may affect subsequent Armillaria disease within high risk stands.

Armillaria Response Tool Light (Stand-Level Version)
ART light assesses potential risk of Armillaria at the stand level as a result of fuels treatment in a stand.
Inputs: Stand location, habitat type
Output: Armillaria Regimes (High or No-to-Low Risk)



Armillaria Response Tool (Landscape-Level Version)
Habitat type and subseries (groups of habitat types) are the backbone of the assessment of Armillaria risk. This more detailed version of ART assesses variability in climate and terrain within forest stands (at the landscape level) to more accurately predict potential risk of Armillaria root rot as a result of fuels treatments.

With the use of GIS and new unbiased plant community classification systems, ART re-examines the historical classification of forested parcels and determines whether some forested parcels have stand heterogeneity and were given a stand-wide plant association label that may encompass two or more ecologically different areas. ART involves GIS analysis of local terrain and climate to reveal such significant within-stand heterogeneity and predicts new habitat assignments for stands. ART assesses that variability to more accurately predict potential risk of Armillaria root rot as a result of fuels treatment in a stand.
Inputs: Stand ID, habitat type, slope, aspect, temperature, precipitation
Output: Armillaria Regimes (High or No-to-Low Risk)
