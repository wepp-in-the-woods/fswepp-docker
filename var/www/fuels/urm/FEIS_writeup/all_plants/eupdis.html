DISTRIBUTION AND OCCURRENCE

SPECIES: Euphrasia disjuncta




GENERAL DISTRIBUTION : 
Arctic eyebright is found in Scandinavia, Iceland, Greenland, Alaska,
and barely into the lower 48 states in Maine, Michigan, Minnesota, and
Montana [4,9].  According to Seymour, this species was reported in
Maine, but no speciman was collected [11].

Occurrence in Glacier National Park:  Upper Lunch Creek Cirque [7].

OTHER USES AND VALUES : 
Arctic eyebright has some potential for use in monitoring for global
change.  Individual plants are small but may be counted unambiguously
[8].


OTHER MANAGEMENT CONSIDERATIONS : 
In Glacier National Park, the fragile habitat of arctic eyebright
should be protected from visitor impact [7].







BOTANICAL AND ECOLOGICAL CHARACTERISTICS

SPECIES: Euphrasia disjuncta




GENERAL BOTANICAL CHARACTERISTICS : 
Arctic eyebright is an exotic annual that grows 1 to 12 inches (3-30
cm) tall.  The leaves grow up to 0.5 inch (1 cm) long.  The capsule is
hairy.  The species is probably a root parasite.  Arctic eyebright
has short-term density fluctuations with high year to year variation
[4,8,9,14].


RAUNKIAER LIFE FORM : 
   Hemicryptophyte


REGENERATION PROCESSES : 
Arctic eyebright reproduces by seed produced by pollination and
fertilization [2].


SITE CHARACTERISTICS : 
Arctic eyebright is a member of a stable tundra community.  It grows
in alpine bogs, moist peaty soil, streambanks, open ground, and other
wet places.  Arctic eyebright occurs at low to high elevations.  It is
common above timberline.  It was reported at 7,200 feet (2,320 m) in
Glacier National Park.  Arctic eyebright grows with hairlike sedge
(Carex cappillaris) and common butterwort (Pinguicula vulgaris).


SUCCESSIONAL STATUS : 
NO-ENTRY


SEASONAL DEVELOPMENT : 
Arctic eyebright flowers in August at high elevations and in July at
lower elevations [4,7].
