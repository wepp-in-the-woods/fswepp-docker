Environmental Consequences Fuels Tools

FuME Fuel Management WEPP FuME estimates background erosion rates and compares sediment loads and erosion from wildfire, thinning, prescribed fire, buffer width effectiveness and low and high use road networks for a given topography. Soil and water databases are the same as those used for WEPP.
Armillaria Response Tool ART models qualitative risk of Armillaria root disease due to selected fuels treatments, determining whether a given habitat supports the disease, and if so, estimating degree of risk.
Wildlife Habitat Response Model WHRM predicts qualitative changes in habitat and populations in response to fuels treatment activities. It uses species-habitat relationships and knowledge of which habitat features a given treatment will affect to identify effects on reproduction, food resources, predators and hazards.
Understory Response Model URM predicts qualitative changes in shrub, form, and grass biomass at 1-, 5-, and 10-year intervals caused by fuels treatment activities, based on species-specific life history traits (life form, shade tolerance, etc) and site-specific effects (soil heating, bare mineral soil, etc.).


Other Tools
  • First Order Fire Effects Model (FOFEM)

  • The Fire and Fuels Extension to the Forest Vegetation Simulator (FFE-FVS)

  • Smoke Impact Spreadsheet Model (SIS)

    SIS calculates 1- and 24-hour particulate matter (PM) emissions and concentrations downwind of fire (wildland, broadcast, pile burns).

  • Fire Effects Planning Framework (FEPF)

  • National Fire Plan Project Design and Consultation Process (FCP)

    FCP provides effects determinations for species listed under the Endangered Species Act and other species of concern for OR, ID, NV, UT, western MT, and western WY.
