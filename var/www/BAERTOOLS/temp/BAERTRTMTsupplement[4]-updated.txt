      <p><a name="DischargeDips"></a>
        <b>Overflow Discharge for Rolling Dips/Water Bars</b>
        (<a href="#Novak2001">Novak and others 2001</a>)</p>
      <p>The overflow discharge over an embankment can be estimated using the following weir formula.</p>
      <p>&nbsp;</p>
      <table width="600" border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
        <tr>
          <td width="40">&nbsp;</td>
          <td colspan="4">
           <span class="style2 style2"><font size="+1"><em>Q = C b H</em></font>
            <font size="+1">
             <em><sup><span style='font-family:Times;mso-bidi-font-family:"Times New Roman"'>3/2</span></sup></em>
            </font>
           </span>
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td width="40">&nbsp;</td>
          <td width="40">&nbsp;</td>
          <td colspan="3">&nbsp;</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td width="40">&nbsp;</td>
          <td width="40">&nbsp;</td>
          <td colspan="3">where</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td width="80" colspan="2">&nbsp;</td>
          <td width="20"><em>Q</em></td>
          <td width="20">=</td>
          <td width="480">overflow discharge (cfs<sup><span style='font-family:Times;mso-bidi-font-family:"Times New Roman"'>)</span></sup>;</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td width="80" colspan="2">&nbsp;</td>
          <td width="20"><i>C</i></td>
          <td width="20">=</td>
          <td width="480">sill coefficient (ft<sup><span style='font-family:Times;mso-bidi-font-family:"Times New Roman"'>1/2
	</span></sup> s<sup>-1</sup>); </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td width="80" colspan="2">&nbsp;</td>
          <td width="20"><em>b</em></td>
          <td width="20">=</td>
          <td width="480">length of the flow section (ft); and,</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td width="80" colspan="2">&nbsp;</td>
          <td width="20"><em>H</em></td>
          <td width="20">=</td>
          <td width="480">total head upstream of the sill (ft).</td>
        </tr>
      </table>
      <p>&nbsp;</p>
      <p>The coefficient of <em>C</em> is a function of <em>h/L</em>
        (<em>h</em> is the head over a sill of width <em>L</em>)
        for free flow or modular flow conditions. Free flow occurs where a man-made structure creates a drop in water level 
        over the structure resulting in the major part of the total upstream energy 
        head being converted into kinetic energy to obtain critical flow at the 
        control section.
        Under this condition, the upstream head is independent of 
        downstream conditions (<a href="#Boiten2002">Boiten 2002</a>).

 The range of values of <i>C</i> for free flow over the embankment is presented below.</p>

      <p>If the drop in water level over the structure becomes rather small, the 
        flow above it remains sub-critical; therefore, the upstream head is affected 
        by downstream conditions.
        This flow type is referred to as submerged flow, non-modular flow, or drowned flow
        (<a href="#Boiten2002">Boiten 2002</a>). 
        In such a case, a correction factor, <em>f</em>, as a function of
        <em>h<sub><span style='font-family:Times;mso-bidi-font-family:"Times New Roman"'>d/s</span></sub>/H</em> 
        (<em>h<sub><span style='font-family:Times;mso-bidi-font-family:"Times New Roman"'>d/s</span></sub></em>is 
        the head drop of a sill to downstream), may be incorporated for submerged 
        flow or non-modular flow conditions.
        The range of values of <i>f</i> for submerged flow over the embankment is presented below.</p>
