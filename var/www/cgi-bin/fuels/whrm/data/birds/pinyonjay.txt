Distribution
interior west from southern Montana to northern New Mexico and Arizona
General Habitat
PIPO forest, pinyon-juniper woodlands
Home or Activity Range
4500 ac (O'Neil et al. 2001)
Canopy Cover
no information available
Specific Habitat Preferences
pinyon pines, PIPO stands for roosting, nesting and foraging
Nestsite Characteristics
colonial nester; nests in branch or fork of tree; nests made of sticks, crumbled bark, grasses, stems, roots, bits of hair, paper
Foraging Habitat Characteristics
feeds on the ground, in understory vegetation and shrubs, and in tree canopy; pine seeds are often cached in the ground for use in winter and spring
Diet
pine seeds, juniper berries, fruits, insects, and occasionally lizards, bird eggs or nestlings; closely tied to pinyon pines outside Oregon; young are fed mostly insects.
Predators
Mexican spotted owl, great horned owl, Accipiter hawks
Shelter Habitats
roost in PIPO trees, using southern exposure

Study Location: northern Arizona
Habitat Description: PIPO
Summary: Pinyon jays roosted primarily on the south sides of pine trees facing west. Sites were selected for thermal economy and predator protection. Jays were observed roosting, nesting and foraging within the confines of the PIPO stand.
