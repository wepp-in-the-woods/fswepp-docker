Distribution
year-round and winter resident across much of USA and Canada, extends into parts of Mexico
General Habitat
breeds in mixed woods; in the west, mainly in mountains

Specific Habitat Preferences
large trees, 40-69% canopy cover
Nestsite Characteristics
nests built on horizontal branch of tree far from the trunk; nest is a frail structure of twigs, grass, moss, roots and pine needles
Foraging Habitat Characteristics
forages in trees, shrubs, and sometimes on the ground
Diet
conifer seeds: fir, pine, spruce, and western red cedar; broad-leafed trees: vine maple, bigleaf maple, dogwood, willow, and oak; insects: (during breeding season) caterpillars, ants, bees, wasps, and true bugs (up to 20% of summer diet); some fruit: mountain ash, chokecherries, juniper berries, and pinion nuts; often consume dirt and gravel for minerals and salts
