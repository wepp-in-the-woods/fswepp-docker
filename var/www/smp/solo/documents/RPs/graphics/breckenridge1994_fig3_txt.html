Long Description of Figure 3:
Flow chart showing links between key processes in Rangelands - expanded from figure 2.

  • Jobs, Recreation and Aesthetics affect:
    1. Riparian Extent, Land Use Patterns, Ecotonal shifts and Fractal dimensions.
    2. Riparian Extent, Land Use Patterns, Ecotonal shifts and Fractal dimensions affect:
    3. Relative abundance, Demographics, Morphological Asymmetry, Habitat and Biomarkers;
    4. Vegetation biomass, Assemblages, diversity, exotics and genetic biomarkers.
  • Soil and Vegetation, Physical Disturbances, Drought, Fire Pathogens, Pollutants and Water Development affect:
    1. Riparian Extent, Land Use Patterns, Ecotonal shifts and Fractal dimensions;
    2. Vegetation biomass, Assemblages, diversity, exotics and genetic biomarkers.
  • Bowens ration, albedo solar radiation, precipitation, wet/dry chemistry and wind affect:
    1. Soil and Vegetation, Physical Disturbances, Drought, Fire Pathogens, Pollutants and Water Development;
    2. Riparian Extent, Land Use Patterns, Ecotonal shifts and Fractal dimensions;
    3. Vegetation biomass, Assemblages, diversity, exotics and genetic biomarkers;
    4. Methane Productions, Foliar and Litter, Chemistry, Soil Chemistry, Soil Energy and Nutrient transport;
    5. Quantity Balance, Quality, Soil Moisture and Historical Water Table Fluctuations.
  • Vegetation biomass, Assemblages, diversity, exotics and genetic biomarkers affect:
    1. Riparian Extent, Land Use Patterns, Ecotonal shifts and Fractal dimensions;
    2. Profile, structure, organic matter, litter, and soil crusts and erosion and disturbance hydrological properties;
    3. Methane Productions, Foliar and Litter, Chemistry, Soil Chemistry, Soil Energy and Nutrient transport;
    4. Microbial and Invertebrate Decomposers, Consumers and Nutrient Cyclers;
    5. Bowens ration, albedo solar radiation, precipitation, wet/dry chemistry and wind.
  • Relative abundance, Demographics, Morphological Asymmetry, Habitat and Biomarkers affect:
    1. Jobs, Recreation and Aesthetics;

  • Microbial and Invertebrate Decomposers, Consumers and Nutrient Cyclers affect:
    1. Vegetation biomass, Assemblages, diversity, exotics and genetic biomarkers;
    2. Methane Productions, Foliar and Litter, Chemistry, Soil Chemistry, Soil Energy and Nutrient transport.
  • Profile, structure, organic matter, litter, and soil crusts and erosion and disturbance hydrological properties affect:

    2. Methane Productions, Foliar and Litter, Chemistry, Soil Chemistry, Soil Energy and Nutrient transport.
