M.U. 10.Ua

MAP UNIT SUMMARY

M.U. SETTING The landform consists of recent floodplains. Map units are

adjacent to perennial streams.

M.U. COMPONENTS Soils include both well and poorly drained types with deep

profiles developed in alluvial parent material. Soils have silty surfaces and

very gravelly sandy loam subsoils. Native vegetation is Douglas fir forests and

fescue meadows.

ADJOINING M.U. Adjacent units are older, higher stream terraces in the broader

stream valleys. In narrow stream valleys, these map units are adjoined by very

steep mountain slopes (streambreaks).

This map unit consists of recent floodplains and is adjacent to perennial

streams and rivers. Streams on these floodplains commonly have braided channels

and change course frequently. These floodplains have fluctuating water tables

and lowest portions of these floodplains flood annually. Because this map unit

occurs adjacent to streams, any soil erosion is likely to become a source of

stream sediment.

VEGETATION

HABITAT TYPE (HT) OCCURRENCE The map unit contains a mosaic of Douglas Series

HTs and grassy meadows on well drained sites. The poorly drained and frequently

flooded soils are occupied by wet sedge meadows and stands of cottonwood.

Dissimilar included HTs are Subalpine fir/queencup beadlily, common horsetail

and western redcedar/queencup beadlily.

HABITAT TYPE DISTRIBUTION Douglas fir Series occur win a mosaic with dry and

wet meadows and cottonwood stands. A relatively warm climate and moderate

timber productivity is associated with this map unit. Seasonal cool air

drainage may occur.

GEOLOGY

OCCURRENCE The unit consists of recent alluvium. The alluvium consists of

stratified sands, gravels, and silts. These bottomlands deposits can be very

thick and are variable in texture. The alluvial gravels are derived primarily by

argillites, siltites, and quartzites from the Belt supergroup.

MANAGEMENT IMPLICATIONS

TIMBER This map unit is moderately suited to timber management. Timber

productivity is moderate; ____ cu.ft/A/yr. Productivity is dependent on the

finer alluvial soils because the coarser aluvial soils are infertile. Slopes

provide for tractor harvest. Soil displacement will reduce site productivity

where a silty surface is removed and the coarse textured subsoil is exposed.

ROADS This map unit is moderately suited to road construction. Shallow ground

water and seasonal flooding can limit construction and increase maintenance.

Flooding and stream channel changes can damage bridges or culverts. The small

areas of deep silty alluvium have low bearing strength and will rut. Rocky

subsoils and substrata produce rough road surfaces. Surfacing improves the

quality of the road.

RANGE This map unit is moderately suited to range management. Potential forage

production is high. Because this map unit contains the highly sensitive riparian

areas, allotment plans should be designed with best practices for riparian

management.

WILDLIFE-FISHERIES Protection from sediment production in streams is critical

for high quality fisheries. ADDITIONAL COMMENTS TO BE OBTAINED BY BIOLOGISTS.

WATERSHED The major watershed management concern is controlling sediment

produced when an activity causing soil disturbance occurs adjacent to the

stream. Stream channel changes can produce high amounts of sediment. Design of

activities away from streamside will promote strambank protection.
