S O I L D E S C R I P T I O N

TYPIC CRYANDEPTS

MEDIAL/LOAMY SKELETAL, MIXED

REFERENCE PEDON. Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise indicated.

O 1-0 ins. - Partially decomposed forest litter

A 0-7 ins. - Dark brown (7.5YR 3/2) silt loam, dark brown (7.5YR 4/2) dry;

weak medium subangular blocky to moderate fine granular structure;

nonsticky and nonplastic; many very fine, common coarse and fine roots; 5

percent gravel; neutral; abrupt wavy boundary.

Bo 7-23 ins. - Strong brown (7.5YR 3/4) silt loam, strong brown (7.5YR 4/6)

dry; moderate medium and fine subangular blocky structure; friable,

nonsticky and nonplastic; common coarse, medium, and fine roots; 5

percent gravel, 5 percent cobble; neutral; clear irregular boundary.

2C 23-40 ins. - Yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) extremely stony silt loam,

brownish yellow (10YR 6/6) dry; moderate medium subangular blocky

structure; firm, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; few fine and

medium roots; 20 percent gravel, 10 percent cobble, 30 percent stone;

slightly acid.

LOCATION AND SETTING: West-central Montana; Bitteroot Mountains; Surveyor's

Creek drainage; center of section 4, T12N, R25W; map unit 42QA. This soil is

formed in volcanic ash influenced loess overlying material derived from glacial

till deposits. The slope gradient is 15 percent on a north aspect at 5,440 feet

of elevation. The habitat type is subalpine fir/queencup beadlily.

These soils are formed in a 14 to 23 inch thick volcanic ash influenced loess

overlying moderately and weakly weathered metasedimentary bedrock and glacial

till deposits.

The A horizon has a silt loam texture, 6 to 8 percent clay, and 0 to 5 percent

coarse fragments. This horizon has other moist colors such as 7.5YR 3/3 and

10YR 2/2.

The Bo horizon has a silt loam texture, 6 to 8 percent clay, and 0 to 15

percent coarse fragments. This horizon has other moist colors such as 10YR 4/4

and 7.5YR 3/3.

2Bw horizons overlay the 2C horizons in some soils. These horizons have silt

loam textures, 10 to 20 percent clay, and 35 to 65 percent coarse fragments.

The 2Bw horizons are 8 to 17 inches thick. Some soils have 2E&2Bw or 2A&2E

horizons in place of a 2Bw horizon.

The 2C horizon has a silt loam to sandy loam texture, 10 to 20 percent clay,

and 50 to 90 percent coarse fragments. This horizon has other moist colors such

as 10YR 6/3 and 10YR 4/6. Depth to the 2C horizon is 23 to 45 inches.
