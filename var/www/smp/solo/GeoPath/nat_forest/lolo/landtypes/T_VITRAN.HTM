S O I L D E S C R I P T I O N #14

TYPIC VITRANDEPTS

MEDIAL/LOAMY SKELETAL, MIXED, FRIGID

REFERENCE PEDON. Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise indicated.

O 2-0 ins. - Moss and roots

A 0-1 ins. - Dark brown (10YR 3/3) gravelly loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4)

dry; moderate medium granular structure; very friable; many fine, few

medium roots; 15 percent gravel; moderately acid; abrupt smooth boundary.

Bo 1-15 ins. - Dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) gravelly silt loam, light

yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) dry; moderate medium granular and weak fine

subangular blocky structure; very friable; common fine and medium roots; 20

percent gravel; moderately acid; diffuse wavy boundary.

2Bw 15-45 ins. - Brown (10YR 5/3) extremely gravelly fine sandy loam, light

gray (10YR 7/2) dry; moderate fine and medium subangular blocky structure;

friable; common fine, few medium and coarse roots; 60 percent gravel, 10

percent cobble; slightly acid.

LOCATION AND SETTING: West-central Montana; Bitterroot Mountains; trubutary to

Clark Fork River, Trout Creek drainage; SE1/4 of NW1/4, section 15, T.15N,

R.26W; map unit 60QD. This soil is formed in a volcanic ash influenced loess

overlying moderately weathered metasedimentary bedrock. The slope gradient is

75 percent on a northeast aspect at 4,560 feet of elevation. The habitat type

is grand fir/kinnikinnick.

These soils are formed in 14 to 25 inch thick volcanic ash influenced loess

overlying material derived from moderately and weakly weathered metasedimentary

bedrock. These soils support moist, mixed coniferous forests.

The Bo horizon has a silt loam texture, 6 to 8 percent clay, and 5 to 20

percent coarse fragments. This horizon has other moist colors such as 10YR 4/3

and 7.5YR 4/4. Some soils have a shallow A horizon.

The 2Bw horizon has a silt loam to sandy loam texture, 5 to 12 percent clay,

and 60 to 80 percent coarse fragments. 2Bw horizons formed in moderately

weathered metasedimentary bedrock are represented by the upper range of clay

percent. This horizon has other moist colors such as 10YR 4/4 and 10YR 6/6.

Some soils have 2E horizons above the 2Bw.
