S O I L D E S C R I P T I O N

ORTHENTS

REFERENCE PEDON. Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise indicated.

A 0-8 ins. - Dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) very gravelly sandy loam, light

yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) dry; weak medium subangular blocky structure;

nonsticky and nonplastic; common fine and medium roots; 50 percent gravel;

neutral; clear smooth boundary.

C1 8-15 ins. - Brown (10YR 4/3) extremely gravelly sandy loam, pale brown

(10YR 6/3) dry; massive to single grained structure; nonsticky and

nonplastic; common medium, few coarse roots; 55 percent gravel, 8 percent

cobbles; slightly acid; abrupt smooth boundary.

C2 15-21+ ins. - Brown (10YR 4/3) extremely cobbly loamy sand, pale brown

(10YR 6/3) dry; single grained structure; nonsticky and nonplastic; 35

percent gravel, 45 percent cobble, 5 percent stone; slightly acid

LOCATION AND SETTING: West-central Montana; adjacent to Clark Fork River; SW

1/4; section 3, T.18N, R.25W; map unit 10UA. This soil is a Typic Xerorthent,

sandy-skeletal and is formed in alluvial deposits. The slope gradient is 2

percent on a west aspect at 2,940 feet of elevation. The habitat type is

Douglas fir/rough fescue.

These soils are formed in alluvial deposits predominantly of sand and gravel

and are excessively drained. Some have volcanic ash influenced loess overlying

the alluvium. They support open grown forests, dry Douglas fir, and dry, mixed

and moist coniferous forests.

Where A surface horizons are present,they have sandy loam textures, 6 to 10

percent clay, and 10 to 50 percent coarse fragments. This horizon has other

moist colors such as 10YR 3/3 and 10YR 4/3. Where Bo surface horizons are

present, they have silt loam textures, 6 to 8 percent clay, and 10 to 25

percent coarse fragments. This horizon has other moist colors such as 10YR 3/6

and 7.5YR 3/4. Some soils have a mixed A horizon which is similar to the

underlying Bo horizon.

The C horizons have sandy loam to sand textures, less than 10 percent clay,

and 15 to 70 percent coarse fragments. These horizons have other moist colors

such as 10YR 4/3 and 10YR 6/3. Colors become variable as textures change from

sandy loam to sand. Depth to the C horizon is 4 to 11 inches.
