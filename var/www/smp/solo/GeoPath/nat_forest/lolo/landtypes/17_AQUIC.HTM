S O I L D E S C R I P T I O N #17

AQUIC CRYOCHREPTS

REFERENCE PEDON. Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise indicated.

0 1-0 ins. - Partially decomposed forest litter

A 0-4 ins. - Dark brown (10YR 3/3) silt loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry; strong

very fine granular structure; firm, slightly sticky and slightly plastic;

many very fine and fine roots; strongly acid; abrupt clear boundary.

Bw 4-16 ins. - Light brown (7.5YR 6/3) gravelly silty clay loam; many large

distinct strong brown (7.5YR 5/6) mottles; strong medium and coarse angular

blocky structure; extremely firm, slightly sticky and plastic; few very

fine and fine roots; 25 percent gravel; strongly acid; clear wavy boundary.

C 16-30 ins. - Brown (7.5YR 5/3) gravelly loam; many large distinct mottles;

massive structure; firm, nonsticky and slightly plastic; 25 percent gravel;

moderately acid.

LOCATION AND SETTING: West-central Montana; Ninemile Divide; Mill Creek; NE1/4

of SE1/4, section 20, T.16N, R.20W; map unit 38QA. This soil is formed in

glacial till deposits. This soil is somewhat poorly drained. The water table

occurs at a depth of 4 to 5 feet. The slope gradient is 10 percent on a

northwest aspect at 5,000 feet of elevation. The habitat type is subalpine

fir/bluejoint.

The A horizon has a silt loam to loam texture and 5 to 25 percent coarse

fragments. This horizon is less than 6 inches thick or can be absent in some

soils.

Some soils have Bo overlying Bw horizons. The Bo horizons have silt loam

textures, 8 to 10 percent clay content, and 5 to 15 percent coarse fragments.

The Bw horizons have silty clay loam to sandy loam textures, up to 35 percent

clay, with 15 to 85 percent coarse fragments. This horizon has common medium

distinct to many large prominant mottles. This horizon has other moist colors

such as 10YR 4/3 and 10YR 5/3.

The C horizons have silty clay loam to loam textures, up to 35 percent clay

content, and 15 to 85 percent coarse fragments. These horizons have mottles.
