MAP UNIT DESCRIPTION

M.U. 10.Ub

MAP UNIT SUMMARY

M.U. SETTING The landform consists of recent floodplains. Map units contain or are adjacent to perennial streams. Beaver ponds are common.

M.U. COMPONENTS Soils are poorly drained with deep profiles developed in alluvial parent material. Soils have deep silty surfaces. Native vegetation is willow and wet sedge bottoms. Low gradient streams cause this map unit to be continually wet.

ADJOINING M.U. Adjacent units are older, higher stream terraces in the broader stream valleys. In narrow stream valleys, these map units are adjoined by very steep mountain slopes (streambreaks).

This map unit consists of recent floodplains and is adjacent to perennial

streams and rivers. Streams on these floodplains commonly have braided channels

and change course frequently. Low gradient meandering streams contribute to

continually wet soils. Because this map unit occurs adjacent to streams, any

soil erosion is likely to become a source of stream sediment.

GEOLOGY

OCCURRENCE The unit consists of recent alluvium. The alluvium consists of

stratified sands, gravels, and silts. These bottomlands deposits can be very

thick and are siltier than most alluvial deposits due to low gradient stream

deposition.

SOILS

GENERAL NATURE OF SOILS This map unit has poorly drained soils due to

continual flooding, channel changes, and high water tables. Surface layers are

contiually changing but generally are silt loams with few gravels.

MANAGEMENT IMPLICATIONS

TIMBER This map unit is poorly suited to timber management. Soils are too wet to

support timber. Perimeters are occupied by spruce and subalpine fir on highly

productive sites. Perimeters, if harvested, are highly susceptable to soil

compaction, rutting, and soil displacement. High water tables will severely

limit access to these sites.

ROADS This map unit is poorly suited to road construction. Shallow ground water

and flooding severely limit construction. Constant stream channel changes and

flooding will increase maintenance on roadbases, bridges, and culverts. Wet

silty alluvium has low bearing strength and will rut.

RANGE This map unit is poorly suited to range management.
