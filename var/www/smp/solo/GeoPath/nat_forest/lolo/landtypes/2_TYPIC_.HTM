S O I L D E S C R I P T I O N

TYPIC CRYOBORALFS

REFERENCE PEDON. Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise indicated.

O 3-0 ins. - Partially decomposed forest litter

Bw1 0-11 ins. - Dark brown (7.5YR 4/4) loam, light brown (7.5YR 6/4) dry; weak

fine and moderate subangular blocky structure; very friable, nonsticky and

nonplastic; common fine and medium, few coarse roots; 5 percent gravel;

strongly acid; clear smooth boundary.

Bw2 11-21 ins. - Light olive brown (2.5Y 5/4) sandy loam, light yellowish

brown (2.5Y 6/4) dry; weak medium subangular blocky structure; very

friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; few fine and coarse roots; 5 percent

gravel; moderately acid; gradual wavy boundary.

Bt1 21-32 ins. - Light olive brown (2.5Y 5/4) and dark yellowish brown (10YR

4/4) coarse sandy loam, light yellowish brown (2.5Y 6/4) and yellowish

brown (10YR 5/4) dry; weak medium subangular blocky structure; friable,

nonsticky and nonplastic; few thin clay films bridging grains; common fine,

few medium roots; 20 percent gravel; neutral;gradual wavy boundary.

Bt2 32-41 ins. - Olive brown (2.5Y 4/4) coarse sandy clay loam, light yellowish

brown (2.5Y 6/4) dry; weak fine subangular blocky structure; firm, slightly

sticky and slightly plastic; few thin clay films bridging grains; few fine

roots; 20 percent gravel; neutral; clear smooth boundary.

C 41-50 ins. - Olive brown (2.5Y 4/4) coarse sandy loam, light yellowish

brown (2.5Y 6/4) dry; massive structure; nonsticky and nonplastic; 40

percent gravel; mildly alkaline.

LOCATION AND SETTING: West-central Montana; Ninemile/Reservation Divide; Mill

Creek; SE1/4 of NW1/4, section 31, T.15N, R.22W; map unit 30PE. This soil is

formed in material derived from gabbroic bedrock. The slope gradient is 35

percent on a north aspect at 5,120 feet of elevation. The habitat type is

subalpine fir/fool's huckleberry.

The Bw horizons have a loam to sandy loam texture, 8 to 20 percent clay, and 5

to 60 percent coarse fragments. This horizon has other moist colors such as

10YR 5/4 and 10YR 4/4. Some soils have an E or shallow A horizon.

The Bt horizons have loam, clay loam, and sandy clay loam textures, 20 to 37

percent clay, and 20 to 50 percent coarse fragments. These horizons have few

thin to many moderately thick clay films lining pores, on ped faces, and

bridging grains. These horizons have another moist color of 10YR 5/4.

The C horizon has a loam to sandy loam texture and 45 to 75 percent coarse

fragments. This horizon have another moist color of 10YR 5/6. Depth to this

horizon is 40 to 60 inches.
