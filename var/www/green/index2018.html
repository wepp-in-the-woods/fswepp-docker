The Rocky Mountain Research Station (RMRS) Sustainable Operations (SusOps) Green Team created this contest to encourage the adoption of sustainable activities in your day-to-day work.
Some actions involve activities at, some at home, and some between.

We encourage you to participate in the Summit Mount Sustainability Footprint Reduction Contest. Participants will compete as individuals and as part of a team primarily based on location, so go out and encourage your co-workers to participate! We all win by learning how to be more sustainable and by reducing our environmental footprint!
  • Participants will work toward one or two two-hour time-off awards.
  • Two group locations will be awarded $250 each for Sustainable Operations improvements of their choice.
To participate, enter your Forest Service e-mail address (or a reasonable facsimile, only letters and numbers allowed) and your duty location, check out the actions, and then return at any time to record the 'green' activities you have completed. Some activities are weekly, some daily, and some are one-time actions within the contest period.

You can visit this web page and check on or correct the recording of your accomplishments, and see how your actions and your location's actions compare to others', at any time during the contest.

If your duty location is not listed, choose the location that your program most closely identifies with – such as your program's headquarters.

Actions for this contest must be recorded by close of business (Mountain Time) Friday, June 8, 2019.

Contest Rules

  • Any RMRS employee (full-time, part-time, temporary, etc.) is eligible to participate. RMRS-hosted employees, students, contractors, etc., may participate as well. However, only RMRS employees are eligible for Time-Off Awards.
  • The contest will run for 6 weeks from April 23 to June 1, 2018.
    • Activities may be reported at any time during the contest. However, only the points reported by 10 am Mountain Time on the Monday following each week will be reported in the official weekly totals.
    • All activities for this contest must be recorded by close of business (Mountain Time) on Friday, June 8, 2018.
  • Participation will be tracked and awarded based on individuals and groups.
    • Individual participants may work toward two separate reward levels.
      • A two-hour time-off award for earning 9,000 or more points by the end of the contest.
      • An additional two-hour time-off award for earning 15,000 or more points by the end of the contest.
    • Group participation is based primarily on contestants' work locations. Award funds must be used for Sustainable Operations improvements. This may include education, renovation, or other projects which promote and encourage sustainable operations growth within RMRS. Participants are required to report a minimum of 500 points a week for at least 3 weeks to be considered a group participant. One group cannot receive both awards.
      • $250 Award for the small group (5-20 possible participants) location with the highest average points earned.
      • $250 Award for the large group (21 or more possible participants) location with the highest average points earned.
  • This contest is based on the honor system – so be truthful and record your activities carefully.
  • Every two weeks, we will post a progress report at each lab location.
