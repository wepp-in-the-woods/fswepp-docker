Characteristics:
Agropyron dasystachyum in Hitchcock et al 1969, St. John 1963, Davis 1953. Agropyron lanceolatum in Piper & Beattie 1914.
What were formerly considered 2 species, Agropyron dasystachyum (Hook.) Scribn. & J.G. Sm. (thickspike wheatgrass) and Agropyron riparium Scribn. & J.G. Sm. (streambank wheatgrass) are now combined as subspecies of E. lanceolatus, E. lanceolatus ssp. lanceolatus (Scribn & J.G. Sm.) Gould and E. lanceolatus ssp. psammophilus Gillett & Senn, respectively.
Reproduces both sexually by seed and vegetatively by rhizomes.
153,000 seeds/lb (USDA NRCS PLANTS Database 2006).

Wind pollinated.
Flowers are perfect.
Fruit is a caryopsis.
Good forage for livestock and wildlife, although Kufeld (1973) rates it of low value for Rocky Mountain elk in spring.
Many grasses are hosts for the larva of the common wood nymph (Cercyonis pegala) and the common ringlet (Coenonympha californica) butterflies (Pocewicz 2005).
