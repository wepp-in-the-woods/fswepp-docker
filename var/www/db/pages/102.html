Genus: Adenocaulon

Variety:
Common Name: pathfinder, American trailplant
Origin: Native to British Columbia south to Oregon and east to Michigan as well as the mountainous regions of California.
Rare: no

Form: forb; slender, erect, deciduous, perennial forb, up to 1 m tall.
Duration: perennial
Longevity:
Habitat Type: forest
Wetland Indicator Status: Not on list.

Leaves: Alternate, mostly basal, triangular to arrowhead-shaped blade, glabrous above, white-woolly below, margins shallowly lobed, 3-15 cm wide with long petioles.
Mature height: to 38 inches
Flowers: Small, white flowers in small heads; June-Sept.
Lacks ray flowers. The outer disc flowers are pistillate, the inner are staminate.
Flower color: white
Bloom: June, July, August, September
Bloom starts on: June
Bloom ends on: September
Fruit: Small, sticky achene, 5-8mm long. Pappus lacking.
Vegetation type: strictly a forest species, rarely found in shrub thickets
Characteristics:
Seeds lack a pappus and are probably dispersed by gravity.
Reproduces both sexually by seed and vegetatively by rhizomes.
Lacks ray flowers. The outer disc flowers are pistillate, the inner are staminate (Hitchcock et al 1969).
Fibrous rooted.
2n=46 (Baldwin et al 2004).
Some native peoples used the plant medicinally.
Comments:

Sun requirement: light to medium shade
Soil moisture: mesic
Precipitation:
Fire: Sprouts from rhizomes near mineral soil suface. Moderately susceptible to fire-kill. May survive moderately severe fires unless they consume the lower duff (Patterson et al 1985).
