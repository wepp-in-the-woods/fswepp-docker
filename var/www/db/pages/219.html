P. smithii is an octoploid (Jones et al 2000). Aneuploidy is known in the species.
Fruit is a caryopsis.
Fair to good forage for wildlife and livestock.
An important forage for Rocky Mountain elk throughout the year (Kufeld 1973). Livestock, large mammals, and rabbits graze the plants. Some birds eat the seeds (Sedivec & Barker 1998).
Many grasses are hosts for the larva of the common wood nymph (Cercyonis pegala) and the common ringlet (Coenonympha californica) butterflies (Pocewicz 2005).
