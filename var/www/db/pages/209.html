Luzula campestris (L.) DC. var. multiflora (Ehrh.) Celak. (LUCAM3).
L. multiflora (Ehrh.) Lej. (LUMU2)
Part of a highly variable complex of species which has a long list of synonyms. Hitchcock et al (1969) calls it “one of the most confusing species in our flora, both morphologically and taxonomically”.
Davis recognized both L. comosa and L. multiflora as separate species, differing in whether the spikes were spreading or stiffly ascending. Daubenmire (1968) followed Davis for taxonomy of the monocots. Following that treatment, L. comosa appeared in several of his Pinus ponderosa-Physocarpus malvaceus and Pinus ponderosa-Symphoricarpos albus plots. According to newer treatments, what is now considered L. comosa is not likely to occur on the Palouse.
L. multiflora (Retz.) Lejeune var. comosa (E. Mey.) in St. John 1963, Juncoides campestre in Piper & Beattie 1914. Davis 1953 recognized both L. comosa Mey. and L. multiflora (Retz.) Lejeune as separate species.
