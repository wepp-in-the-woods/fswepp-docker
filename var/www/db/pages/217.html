Plants are root parasites, usually of Sedum or members of Asteraceae and Saxifragaceae. They have only rudimentary leaves and lack chlorophyll, emerging above ground only to flower.
Plants on Kamiak Butte are apparently parasitic on Lomatium macrocarpum and Eriogonum heracleoides (Hufford 1997).
