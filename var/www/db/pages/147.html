Notes: Collomia linearis is an annual. Plants growing under low levels of competition grow larger and bloom for a longer time than those one usually sees in the wild. It dries up earlier than C. grandiflora and is not as large. Flowers are a run-of-the-mill pink and smaller than C. grandiflora. It also expels its seed explosively, so can move around rapidly. Can be sown in spring, does better with fall seeding. Common names include narrow-leaved collomia, tiny trumpet (Skinner et al 2005).
