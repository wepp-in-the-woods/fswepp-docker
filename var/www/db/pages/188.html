Characteristics:
Differs from H. cynoglossum primarily by being more setose and lacking an obviously glandular involucre. The two species grade into one another and are sometimes considered a single species. H. albertinum also grades into H. scouleri and may be classified as a variety of H. scouleri. Other authors combine H. albertinum and H. cynoglossum as H. cynoglossum.
727,156 seeds/lb (USDA NRCS Pullman Plant Materials Center 2005).
