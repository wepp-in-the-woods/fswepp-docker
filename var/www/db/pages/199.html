Characteristics:
Currently treated as part of L. glabrum or sometimes as a variety or subspecies thereof.
L. bulbifera is synonymous. Apparently “phragma”, meaning wall, fence, or partition, is of neuter gender and requires the ending “um” rather than the feminine ending “a”. The “a” ending was chosen by the original author and is often used, while other floras chose to correct it to the neuter ending.
Tellima tenella in Piper & Beattie 1914, L. bulbiferum in Davis 1953, L. glabra in St. John 1963.
Reproduces both sexually by seed and vegetatively by bulblets borne both in the leaf axils and on the roots.

The bulblets of L. parviflorum are eaten by rodents and game birds (Craighead et al 1963). It is quite likely that they also consume the bulblets of L. bulbiferum.
