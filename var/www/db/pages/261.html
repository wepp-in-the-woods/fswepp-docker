Other Propagation Information:
Sedums are easy to propagate vegetatively. A small piece of leafy stem will usually root (Parish et al 1996).
Germinate at a constant 15oC in light or dark, or with alternating temperatures of 15-20oC with light. Fresh seed has some dormancy which is lost after 6 months of dry storage (Sayers & Ward 1966).
Reproduces both sexually by seed and vegetatively by rhizomes. Sometimes produces bulbils in place of flowers. Sedums can be propagated by division or from seed.
