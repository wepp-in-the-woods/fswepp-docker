Remarks: remarks
Notes: Aster occidentalis is a late blooming plant. Weakly rhizomatous, but its primary means of reproduction is seed, which it produces in prodigious quantities. Seeds germinate readily. It can be weedy, which makes it a good choice for a wilder natural but probably not a good plant in a small, formal garden. Flowers in mid to late July. Needs late season moisture. South facing slopes are too dry and hot. Common name is western aster (Skinner et al 2005).
