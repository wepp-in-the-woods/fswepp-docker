Plant Species: Philadelphus lewisii, Lewis' mockorange

Kingdom: Plantae
Phylum: Magnoliophyta -- flowering plants
Class: Magnoliopsida -- dicotyledons
Family: Hydrangeaceae
Genus: Philadelphus
Species: lewisii
Variety:
Common Name: Lewis’ mockorange
Species Code: PHLE4
Origin: Native to dry to moist places, especially canyons, talus slopes, riparian areas, and dry open forests of British Columbia, Washington, Oregon, California, Idaho, and Montana.
Rare: no

Form: shrub, deciduous, rounded to erect, 1.5-3 m tall, loosely branched, bark reddish-brown, turning grey with age and eventually flaking, twigs glabrous to hairy.
Duration: perennial
Longevity:
Habitat Type: talus slopes, prairie, shrub thickets, forest
Wetland Indicator Status: not listed

Leaves: opposite, petiolate, ovate, to elliptic, 3-5 cm long, margins entire to remotely denticulate, strigose to glabrous, scaberulous, with 3 prominent veins from the base.
Mature height: 5-16 feet
Flowers: fragrant, showy, 4-merous, 3-11 in terminal racemes on lateral branches; calyx usually glabrous or sometimes strigose, turbinate to campanulate, 4-7 mm long, the lobes ovate to ovate-lanceolate, acuminate; petals white, from ovate to oblong to nearly obovate, mostly 10-20 mm long, obtuse or rounded, stamens numerous, unequal.
Flower color: white
Bloom: June, July
Bloom starts on: June 22
Bloom ends on: July 30
Fruit: capsule, woody, ovoid-elliptic, 6-10 mm long; seeds 2.5-3 mm long, fusiform, brown.
Vegetation type:
Characteristics:
Reproduces sexually by seed.
Usually placed in the family Hydrangeaceae but some treatments place it in Philadelphaceae.
8,000,000 seeds/lb (USDA NRCS PLANTS Database 2009).
2n=26.
Diploids only are know.
Flowers are perfect.
Fruit is a capsule.
Native peoples used the stems for making tools and weapons.
The shrubs are good cover for wildlife. Deer and elk browse the plants.
An important browse plant for Rocky Mountain elk in winter (Kufeld 1973).

Other Propagation Information:
Seeds need 8 weeks cold moist stratification at 5oC then germinate at 22-26oC (Rose et al 1998).
Some germination occurs without pretreatment, but cold moist stratification increases germination (Mirov 1939).
Can be propagated from summer softwood with 0.1% IBA and mist. Hardwood cuttings can also be rooted, but success rates are lower (USDA NRCS Pullman Plant Materials Center 1985 Annual Technical Report p. 447).
Seed requires 5 months of cold moist stratification in mesh bags between layers of peat (Hudson & Carlson 1986).
Seed is stored in the seedbank (Kramer & Johnson 1987).
Seed requires 3-8 weeks cold moist stratification. Germination is inhibited by total light or total darkness. Softwood cuttings root treated with 1,000 ppm IBA root under mist. Hardwood cuttings should be treated with 2,500 or 8,000 ppm IBA (Shaw et al undated in Woody Plant Seed Manual).
Reproduces sexually by seed.
