Characteristics:
Triteleia grandiflora Lindl var. grandiflora is the preferred name. Hookera douglasii in Piper & Beattie 1914.
Reproduces sexually by seed and vegetatively by offsets of the corms.
Perennating organ is a corm.
2n = 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, & 56 (Barkworth 1977).
Flowers are perfect.
Diploids and polyploids exist. Polyploids produce more cormlets than diploids and usually occupy sites where the soil is unstable. Diploids prefer flatter areas or areas where other vegetation is more dense (Barkworth 1977).
Corms are edible.
Fruit is a capsule.
Bears (Craighead et al 1963) and other wildlife eat the corms. Deer browse the foliage.
White flowered forms may occasionally be found.
