Characteristics:
P. picta, P. asarifolia, P. dentata, and P. virens (P. clorantha) all may have leafless forms. Since the different species are primarily distinguished by the morphology of the leaves, leafless forms are often referred to P. aphylla, although P. aphylla is not a true species. Camp (1940) placed most of the leafless forms in P. picta. He found both leafed and leafless forms attached to the same rhizome. Haber (1987) confirmed Camp’s work and further included P. dentata in P. picta. Through molecular analysis, Freudenstein (1997) placed P. aphylla and P. picta in the same clade.
Some treatments place Pyrola in the family Ericaceae, subfamily Pyroloideae. Others elevate Pyroloideae to family status as Pyrolaceae.
Reproduces both sexually by seed and vegetatively by rhizomes.
