Characteristics:
L. parviflora is synonymous. Apparently “phragma”, meaning wall, fence, or partition, is of neuter gender and requires the ending “um” rather than the feminine ending “a”. The feminine ending was chosen by the original author and is often used, while other floras chose to correct it to the neuter ending.
Tellima parviflora in Piper & Beattie 1914, L. parviflorum in Davis 1953. L. parviflora in Hitchcock & Cronquist 1973 and in St. John 1963.
Despite the fact that "parviflorum" means “small flowered”, the flowers are larger than those of L. bulbiferum, with which it is often sympatric.
Pollinated by bombyliid flies, solitary bees, and the moth Greya politella (Thompson and Pellmyr 1992). The females of Greya politella effect pollination while ovipositing in the flower. The resulting larva consume some of the seeds (Thompson & Pellmyr 1992, Pellmyr et al 1996).
Reproduces both sexually by seed and vegetatively by bulblets borne on the roots.
