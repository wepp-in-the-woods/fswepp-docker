Common Name: slender wheatgrass
Origin: Native to North America. Widespread in moist meadows and open forest over much of North America except the southeastern US. There is some question as to whether slender wheatgrass is native to the Palouse. It is a native of Montana which has been widely planted for forage, hay, erosion control, and green manure and it is considered likely that occurrences on the Palouse are the result of those introductions.

Excellent forage for livestock and wildlife.
Low value forage for Rocky Mountain elk in summer, better in fall (Kufeld 1973). Good to excellent forage for livestock and wildlife, especially early in the season (Stubbendieck 1997). Small mammals and large ungulates forage of the plants during all seasons (Sedivec & Barker 1998).
Many grasses are hosts for the larva of the common wood nymph (Cercyonis pegala) and the common ringlet (Coenonympha californica) butterflies (Pocewicz 2005).
