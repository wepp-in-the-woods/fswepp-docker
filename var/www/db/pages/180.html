Other Propagation Information:
Disc achenes germinate at high rates over a broad temperature range while ray flower achenes germinate best at alternating day/night temperatures of 22/17oC (McDonough 1969).
Ray achenes have a narrower range of optimum temperatures than disc achenes for germination. Germination of both types is increased by cold moist stratification and by exposure to light. Seeds probably do not survive in the seed bank beyond the next spring. Plants require vernalization to flower (McDonough 1975).
Germination in alternating light/dark at 15oC was only 8% (Maguire & Overland 1959).
