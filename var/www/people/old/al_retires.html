Al Harvey retires December 31, 1999

Project Leader Al Harvey, Moscow, has announced his retirement on December 31, 1999. Al started his career with the FS in 1965 as a Research Plant Pathologist at the Intermountain Station's Moscow lab, working exclusively on white pine blister rust. He developed a method to culture this important parasite, and demonstrated its critical needs for survival in the absence of its host and its ability to establish connections with the white pine even without direct physical contact.

Al transferred to Missoula in 1973 where he supported the Forest Residues and Prescribed Fire R&D Programs. There, he concentrated on the potential impacts of the application of intensive utilization and prescribed fire practices to interior western forest ecosystems.

In 1979, he was "cycled" back to Moscow where he worked as Project Leader for the Pathology Unit. He served in that capacity through many iterations of the Project, mostly recently titled "Microbial Processes as Ecosystem Regulators in Western Forests." Al and his unit have been developing a broad ecological understanding of the contributions of microbial processes to western forest ecosystems, to their interactions with physical disturbances such as wildfire and harvesting, and to their direct participation in long-term site and soil limitations.

Al has authored or coauthored approximately 175 professional publications. Following retirement, he plans to finish up things he was not able to get done in recent years. "It's time to place priority on family, travel and a more relaxed life," he said.

Source: Rocky Mountain Station "Director's Notes" December 27, 1999
